# El Viñedo — Brand Guidelines

> Wine degustation event | June 27, 2024 | Manta, Ecuador

---

## 1. Brand Voice & Tone

### Core Personality
El Viñedo speaks like your well-traveled friend who discovered wine and can't stop sharing it — enthusiastic, unpretentious, and always making you feel like you belong. Never a lecture. Always an invitation.

**The Golden Rule:** If it sounds like a sommelier wrote it, rewrite it.

### Tone Spectrum
```
FUN/SOCIAL ←————————————————→ INFORMATIVE/EDUCATIONAL
    |                                    |
"Come hang with us"          "Here's what to expect"
    ↑                                    ↑
Posts 1–2 weeks before event      Awareness phase posts
```

Balance: **60% social/fun** | **40% educational/informative**

### Language TO USE ✅

| Phrase Style | Example |
|---|---|
| Conversational invitations | "No wine knowledge? Perfect. That's literally why we're doing this." |
| Discovery framing | "You might find your new favorite drink here." |
| Friend-to-friend tone | "Think of it as a fun Saturday out — with really good drinks." |
| Demystifying language | "Red, white, rosé — we'll walk you through all of it, no test at the end." |
| Inclusive humor | "Wine experts welcome. But honestly, beginners have more fun." |
| Local pride | "Manta deserves a proper wine experience." |
| FOMO-lite | "Limited spots — the people who went to the last one are already asking about the next." |

### Language TO AVOID ❌

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "Terroir", "tannins", "bouquet", "palate" | Jargon that alienates beginners |
| "For the connoisseur..." | Signals exclusivity, not inclusion |
| "Premium experience" (overused) | Vague corporate speak |
| "An evening of sophistication" | Too stiff for Manta's social culture |
| Excessive formality: "We cordially invite you" | Kills the casual vibe |
| Wine ratings / scores (94 points, etc.) | Meaningless to target audience |
| "Only for true wine lovers" | Directly excludes the target market |

## 2. Target Audience Deep Dive

### Persona Profiles

#### Persona 1: "Gabriela" — The Social Organizer
- **Age:** 34 | **Job:** Marketing manager at a real estate firm
- **Income:** $2,500–4,000/month
- **Social style:** Plans group outings, loves Instagram-worthy experiences
- **Motivation:** Find a unique activity to do with her friend group; something different from the usual bar scene
- **Wine knowledge:** Drinks wine at restaurants, knows "I like rosé" but nothing else
- **Objections:** "Is it going to be boring? Will I feel dumb if I don't know anything?"
- **What convinces her:** Photos of people laughing and having fun (not standing stiffly with glasses)
- **How she discovers it:** Instagram Stories, tagged by friends

#### Persona 2: "Carlos" — The Aspiring Professional
- **Age:** 42 | **Job:** Business owner / entrepreneur
- **Income:** $5,000+/month
- **Social style:** Attends events for networking; values experiences that signal good taste
- **Motivation:** Expand his palate, have something to talk about at client dinners, meet like-minded people
- **Wine knowledge:** Occasionally orders wine at nicer restaurants, feels slightly self-conscious about not knowing more
- **Objections:** "Is $X worth it? Will it actually teach me something practical?"
- **What convinces him:** Clear value proposition, mention of what he'll learn, prestige angle
- **How he discovers it:** Facebook, word of mouth from peers

#### Persona 3: "Valeria & Group" — The Weekend Experience Hunters
- **Age:** 30–38 | **Mix:** Couples, friend groups
- **Income:** Combined household $3,000–5,000/month
- **Social style:** Always looking for weekend plans that are "something different"
- **Motivation:** Shared experience, memory-making, getting out of routine
- **Wine knowledge:** Zero to minimal — wine is just "the thing at dinner"
- **Objections:** "Cost for two? Is this for serious wine people only?"
- **What convinces them:** Group pricing mention, casual fun atmosphere, "bring your friends" framing
- **How they discover it:** Instagram feed posts, Facebook events

### Pain Points & Objections Map

| Objection | Frequency | Counter-Message |
|---|---|---|
| "I don't know anything about wine" | HIGH | "That's the point — we'll teach you everything, starting from zero" |
| "It might be expensive" | HIGH | "One evening, everything included — you'll spend more at a restaurant" |
| "It sounds snobby/not for me" | HIGH | Show real people having fun, not formal wine settings |
| "I don't have time" | MEDIUM | "3 hours on a Saturday. That's it." |
| "I'm not a wine person" | MEDIUM | "Neither were we, until we went to one of these" |
| "June is busy" | LOW | Build FOMO early; lock in tickets before schedules fill |

## 3. Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement
> El Viñedo is Manta's most social wine experience — designed for people who are curious about wine but don't want to feel judged for not knowing it yet.

### Why Attend? (Core Narrative)
Not a class. Not a formal tasting with white tablecloths and silence. El Viñedo is a Saturday where you try 8–12 wines, learn the basics without jargon, meet cool people from Manta, and leave with at least one new favorite bottle you can actually find in a local store.

### Differentiation from "Stuffy" Wine Events
| Generic Wine Event | El Viñedo |
|---|---|
| Expert-led, top-down lecture | Guided but conversational, questions welcome |
| Formal dress code implied | Smart casual, come as you are |
| Wine jargon throughout | Plain language only |
| Intimidating for beginners | Literally designed for beginners |
| Isolated from local commerce | Connects to local stores you can shop at after |

### Core Value Propositions (3–5)

1. **Learn without the pressure** — Walk in knowing nothing, walk out knowing enough to impress at your next dinner.
2. **A social event first, wine tasting second** — Meet interesting Manta locals over great drinks.
3. **Discover wines you can actually buy locally** — We partner with local stores so you can take your favorites home.
4. **Experience over expertise** — No tests, no grades, no judgment. Just taste and decide what you like.
5. **Worth every centavo** — An all-inclusive evening with expert guidance, multiple wines, and a good crowd.

## 4. Visual Identity Guidelines

### Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| Deep Wine/Burgundy | `#722F37` | Primary brand color, headers, accents |
| Warm Gold | `#D4A843` | Highlights, CTAs, premium feel without being cold |
| Cream/Off-white | `#F5F0E8` | Backgrounds, clean space |
| Terracotta | `#C4692A` | Warmth, Mediterranean feel, secondary accent |
| Charcoal | `#2D2D2D` | Body text, not pure black (softer) |
| Sage Green | `#7A8C6E` | Occasional nature/vineyard accent |

### Photography Style

**DO capture:**
- Groups of 3–5 friends laughing with glasses in hand (outdoor or warmly lit indoor)
- Close-ups of wine being poured into a glass (movement, liquid motion)
- Overhead flat lays: wine bottle + cheese + glasses on warm wood surface
- Candid learning moments: someone smelling a glass, listening to a presenter, looking surprised (in a good way)
- Manta coastal elements in background where possible (lifestyle connection)
- Diverse group of Manteños — mix of ages 28–50, professional but relaxed

**AVOID:**
- Stiff posed photos with people looking directly at camera holding glasses
- Overly formal settings (white tablecloths, formal attire)
- Stereotypical sommelier imagery (snobbish tasting stance)
- Stock photo aesthetic — must feel authentic
- Pure white backgrounds (too clinical)

### Typography Direction

| Element | Direction |
|---|---|
| Headlines | Modern serif with warmth (e.g., Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond) |
| Body/Captions | Clean sans-serif (e.g., Lato, Nunito) |
| Accent text | Italic serif for quotes and highlights |
| Avoid | Overly decorative scripts that are hard to read on mobile |

### Instagram Aesthetic Direction
- **Color temperature:** Warm — golden hour tones, amber lighting
- **Filter style:** Slight desaturation with warm highlight boost (not HDR, not cold/blue)
- **Feed consistency:** Alternate between lifestyle photos, text/graphic posts, and product shots
- **Grid pattern suggestion:** Every 3rd post is a graphic/text card to break up photography

## 5. CTAs and Messaging Framework

### Primary CTA: Buy Tickets

**Phase 1 — Awareness (Months 1–2): Soft CTAs**
Goal: Build curiosity, not pressure

| CTA Copy | Use |
|---|---|
| "Save this for June 27 🍷" | Instagram save prompt |
| "Tell us: red or white?" | Engagement question |
| "Follow for the full lineup" | Follower growth |
| "Tag someone who loves wine (or thinks they might)" | Reach expansion |

**Phase 2 — Consideration (Month 2–3): Mid CTAs**
Goal: Move from awareness to intent

| CTA Copy | Use |
|---|---|
| "Grab your spot before they go" | Urgency without panic |
| "June 27 — are you in?" | Direct question CTA |
| "Link in bio for tickets" | Conversion |
| "Spots are limited — just saying" | Soft scarcity |

**Phase 3 — Conversion (Final 2 weeks): Hard CTAs**
Goal: Close the sale

| CTA Copy | Use |
|---|---|
| "Last few tickets. June 27, Manta." | Direct urgency |
| "Don't miss it — get your ticket now [link]" | Direct |
| "Bringing a friend? Group tickets available" | Bundle prompt |

### Secondary CTA: Visit Partner Liquor Stores

**Approach:** Frame as a benefit, not an ad
- "Love what you tasted? Find it at [Store Name] — they stock the full lineup."
- "Our friends at [Partner Store] have the bottles from tonight's tasting. Stop by."
- "Want to practice before June 27? [Partner Store] has a tasting kit ready for you."

### CTA Rules
- **Never use**: "Buy now!", "Don't wait!", "Act fast!" — too pushy for this audience
- **Always pair CTAs with a reason**: Not just "get tickets" but "get tickets + here's what you'll experience"
- **One CTA per post** — don't ask for two actions in the same post
- **Link in bio** for Instagram (no swipe-up unless Reels/Stories with 10k+ followers)
