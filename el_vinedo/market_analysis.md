## 1. Social Media Landscape (Manta, Ecuador)

### Instagram & Facebook Trends for Mid-High Income 30–50 Demographic in Coastal Ecuador

The 30–50 age bracket in Manta's mid-high income segment is digitally active but selective. Key behavioral traits:

- They use social media for social signaling, lifestyle aspiration, and local discovery.
- Facebook remains dominant for event discovery and RSVPs; the older edge of the bracket (45–50) checks Facebook daily.
- Instagram is used for visual inspiration and status-affirming content — they engage with aspirational but locally relevant imagery.
- WhatsApp is the primary private communication channel. People routinely share event posts from Instagram/Facebook into WhatsApp groups. This secondary amplification is critical and cost-free.
- They respond to polished aesthetics that feel upscale but not cold or international. Warmth, local color, and real people outperform sterile studio shots.
- Educational content ("learn something new") performs exceptionally well with this demographic because the target has limited wine knowledge and curiosity is high.

### Content Types by Performance

| Content Type | Performance Level | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram Reels (15–30 sec) | Highest reach | Algorithm-favored; best for awareness phase |

**Recommendation:** Lead with Reels for reach and Carousels for depth. Use Stories daily in the final 10 days before the event. A Facebook Events page is non-negotiable for this demographic.

### Competitor Analysis: Food/Wine/Lifestyle Events in Ecuador

**What works in the Ecuadorian market:**

- **Premium venue photography:** Events at Hotel Oro Verde Guayaquil and similar properties generate strong engagement when the venue aesthetics are front and center. The venue signals legitimacy and exclusivity before the audience reads a word.
- **Guest-centric storytelling:** Posts featuring real attendees in candid moments outperform posed product shots. The audience identifies with people who look like them.
- **Educational hooks:** "¿Sabías que...?" (Did you know?) wine facts in captions drive saves and shares. The no-wine-knowledge audience responds strongly to approachable education.
- **Behind-the-scenes content:** Setup footage, curation process, tasting notes being written — this builds anticipation and trust before the event.
- **Active Facebook Event host responses:** Hosts who answer questions in comments convert significantly more RSVPs than those who let comments go unanswered.

**What fails in this market:**
- Generic wine imagery (stock photo glasses on marble) with no local context.
- Overly formal or snobbish copy that makes the event feel inaccessible to non-wine-drinkers.
- Posting only in the week before the event — insufficient warm-up period destroys potential reach.
- Ignoring WhatsApp amplification (not explicitly asking followers to share to their WhatsApp groups).
- Ticket pricing displayed without value anchoring — no mention of how many wines, whether food is included, what the experience involves.

**Key comparable events to monitor:**
- Casa del Vino (Quito and Guayaquil) — educational-first Instagram approach
- Vinoteca (Guayaquil) — strong Facebook Events presence
- Hotel Oro Verde Manta's food and beverage events
- Manta restaurants hosting "noche de vinos" (wine nights)

### TikTok Viability Assessment

**Verdict: Low priority. Allocate maximum 10–15% of content effort. Do not allocate paid budget.**

Reasoning:
- The 30–50 mid-high income demographic in Manta has significantly lower TikTok penetration than Instagram/Facebook. Nationally, TikTok Ecuador skews 18–28.
- TikTok content creation requires a distinct production style (trending sounds, native editing pace, faster cuts) that requires separate effort from Instagram Reels — stretching an already lean budget.
- TikTok's local geo-targeting for Manta specifically is weak compared to Meta's tools.
- **Exception:** If a Reel can be repurposed as a TikTok with minimal editing (same video, different caption and tags), post it. Cross-posting costs nothing and may capture younger tourists browsing locally.
- **If posting on TikTok:** Keep videos under 30 seconds, use trending audio, focus on visually dramatic shots (pouring wine, swirling glass, venue reveal). Do not invest paid budget.

## 2. Political / Economic / Seasonal Context

### Ecuador's Economic Environment (2024) and Discretionary Spending

Ecuador in 2024 is in a post-pandemic recovery phase combined with political instability following the 2023 extraordinary elections. The elected government under President Daniel Noboa has taken a broadly business-friendly posture, which has partially stabilized consumer and investor sentiment.

**Impact on mid-high income discretionary spending:**
- The mid-high income bracket in Manta (roughly $2,500–$6,000/month household income) has been relatively insulated from economic shocks that affected lower-income groups.
- Ecuador is fully dollarized — no currency risk or inflation concerns for this demographic.
- Fuel subsidy adjustments in 2024 mildly increased cost of living but not enough to suppress occasional luxury experiences for this bracket.
- **Key insight for pricing and messaging:** This bracket is willing to spend on experiences they perceive as special and socially shareable. Frame the ticket as "an experience" — not a cover charge. Recommended ticket price: $35–$55 per person for a 3-hour degustation with 6–8 wines and food pairings. Above $70 requires stronger value justification.
- Mention cuotas (credit card installment options) if the ticketing platform supports it — this removes price friction for a meaningful segment.

### Tourism Season Analysis: June in Manta

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| Season type | Shoulder season — garúa (coastal mist) begins; beach tourism dips vs. Dec–April peak |
| Domestic tourism | Strong — Ecuadorians from Quito/Cuenca/Guayaquil visit Manta as highland cold intensifies |
| International tourism | Lower volume than high season; quality visitors with higher spending tend to stay longer |
| Hotel occupancy estimate | 50–65% in June vs. 80–95% December–April |

**Primary tourist nationalities visiting Manta in June:**
1. Ecuadorian domestic visitors from Quito, Guayaquil, and Cuenca — largest segment by far
2. Colombian tourists (short-haul, growing market)
3. US expats and retirees resident in Ecuador (many Cuenca-based expats travel to coast)
4. European backpackers — low spending, not target demographic
5. Cruise ship passengers — opportunistic when ships call at Puerto Manta

**Tourist opportunity:** The domestic Quito/Guayaquil visitor is excellent secondary target. They are mid-high income, curious about local cultural experiences, and actively seeking evening activities. Instagram and Facebook geo-targeted ads toward people physically in Manta hotels during June are worth $100–150 of the total ad budget.

### June Seasonal Factors

**Weather:** June marks the start of garúa season — overcast skies, light coastal mist, temperatures 19–23°C (66–73°F). Cooler and drier than summer peak. This is an advantage for an indoor evening wine event. Lean into the weather in copy: "The perfect evening to come inside and discover great wine."

**School calendar:** Ecuador's coastal school year (Costa region, which includes Manta) runs April through January. June 27 is mid-school-year. Families with school-age children need childcare arrangements on a weeknight — consider a "Date Night" framing or a couples ticket bundle to pre-address this objection.

**Local holidays in June 2024 (Ecuador):**
- June 5: World Environment Day (not an official holiday)
- June 16: Father's Day (widely celebrated, not a public holiday)
- June 24: Inti Raymi / San Juan (significant in the Sierra highlands; minimal observance on the coast)
- No national public holidays fall on or adjacent to June 27 — the date is clean.

### Regulatory Considerations for Alcohol/Wine Events in Ecuador

- **Venue license:** Events serving alcohol require permits from ARCSA (Agencia Nacional de Regulación, Control y Vigilancia Sanitaria) and local municipal approval. The venue operator typically holds this license — confirm before signing the venue contract that their existing license covers private event alcohol service.
- **Legal drinking age:** 18 years in Ecuador. Not relevant for this demographic but include "18+" language in all ticketing and advertising.
- **Service hours:** Manta's municipal regulations allow alcohol service until 02:00. A degustation event ending by 22:00–23:00 faces zero regulatory risk.
- **Import duties:** Chilean and Argentinian wines carry import duties under Ecuador's COMEX tariff schedule. This affects the price you pay from importers — factor it into wine cost negotiations but it does not affect event organizer compliance.
- **Social media alcohol advertising:** Meta requires age-gating on paid ads for alcohol content. When setting up boosted posts or ad campaigns, set the age restriction to 18+ in the ad targeting. This is an ad setup step, not a content creation requirement.

### Instagram: Best Days (Ranked for Manta Mid-High Income 30–50 Demographic)

| Rank | Day | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Wednesday** | Mid-week peak; professionals mentally shift mid-week and scroll more; highest event discovery mode |
| 2 | **Thursday** | "Pre-weekend" energy; people start planning Thursday and Friday evening; high intent engagement |
| 3 | **Tuesday** | Strong secondary day; Monday recovery is over; audience receptive to non-urgent lifestyle content |
| 4 | **Friday** | Weekend mindset; strong for urgency and reminder posts ("this week," "tomorrow night") |
| 5 | **Monday** | Decent for reach; good for "week ahead" awareness framing; lower emotional engagement than Wed–Thu |
| 6 | **Saturday** | Lower for this demographic (family and social activities offline); acceptable for morning posts only |
| 7 | **Sunday** | Lowest engagement; this demographic is family-oriented on Sundays; avoid primary promotional posts |

### Instagram: Best Times (ECT — Ecuador Central Time, UTC-5)

| Time Window | Quality | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **12:00–14:00 ECT** | Excellent | Lunch break peak; office workers on phone; highest mid-day engagement for 30–50 demo |
| **19:30–21:30 ECT** | Excellent | Evening wind-down; highest total engagement window; aspirational content resonates here |
| **07:30–08:30 ECT** | Good | Morning commute; shorter engagement sessions; Stories and Reels perform better than Carousels |
| **15:00–16:30 ECT** | Moderate | Afternoon slump; decent but not worth prioritizing |
| **21:30–23:00 ECT** | Low-Moderate | Late evening engagement drops; the 30–50 bracket trends toward earlier sleep than younger demographics |

**Primary Instagram recommendation:** Post Wednesday or Thursday at **12:30 ECT** or **20:00 ECT**.

### Facebook: Best Days and Times

The 30–50 demographic uses Facebook more habitually and less aspirationally than Instagram. Facebook is where they discover events, engage with community content, and RSVP. Behavior differs meaningfully from Instagram.

| Day | Best Time (ECT) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Tuesday** | 13:00–14:00 | Strong mid-week Facebook discovery window; less competition than Wednesday |
| **Wednesday** | 09:30–10:30 and 13:00–14:00 | Peak day; event shares and comments concentrate here |
| **Thursday** | 19:00–21:00 | Weekend planning begins; Facebook Events get actively checked Thursday evenings |
| **Friday** | 08:00–09:30 | Early morning Facebook check; "what's happening this weekend" browsing behavior |
| **Saturday** | 10:00–12:00 | Late morning leisure browsing; shares peak as people recommend things to their networks |
| **Sunday** | Avoid | Significantly lower engagement; family focus takes priority |
| **Monday** | Avoid for primary posts | Lower engagement; reserve for supplementary or evergreen content only |

**Facebook-specific note:** Facebook Events with active host engagement (answering every comment and question within 2 hours) convert RSVPs at a measurably higher rate than Instagram event links for this age bracket. Native Facebook video outperforms shared Instagram links — upload video files directly.

### Is 2x/Week Optimal? Campaign Phase Cadence

No — cadence should increase progressively as the event approaches. A flat 2x/week schedule wastes the algorithm's compounding momentum.

| Phase | Timeline | Frequency | Platforms | Content Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | June 3–14 (4–3 weeks out) | 2x/week Instagram + 1x/week Facebook | Educational Reels and Carousels | Build audience familiarity; establish account credibility; wine education content |
| **Engagement** | June 14–21 (2 weeks out) | 3x/week Instagram + 2x/week Facebook + daily Stories | Partner features, ticket availability, social proof | Consideration phase; move warm audience toward ticket purchase |
| **Urgency/Conversion** | June 21–26 (final week) | Daily Instagram Stories + 2x/week feed posts + daily Facebook | Countdown, limited tickets, influencer content, partner reshares | Drive ticket sales; last-chance messaging |
| **Event Day** | June 27 | Live Stories every 45–60 minutes + 1 feed post | Instagram Stories, optional Facebook Live | Real-time FOMO for non-attendees; content archive for future events |
| **Post-Event** | June 28–July 3 | 2–3 posts total | Recap Reel, thank-you post, testimonials | Community building; soft signal for a future event |

### Platform-Specific Posting Differences

| Factor | Instagram | Facebook |
|---|---|---|
| Best posting frequency | 3x/week feed + daily Stories in final 10 days | 2x/week + Event page updates |
| Peak engagement window | 12:00–14:00 and 19:30–21:30 ECT | 09:30–11:00 and 19:00–21:30 ECT |
| Content format priority | Reels > Carousels > Stories > Static | Native video > Static post > Shared links |
| Event discovery mechanism | Explore page, hashtags, Reels algorithm | Facebook Events, Group shares, News Feed |
| Hashtag count | 10–15 per post; varied rotation | 2–4 hashtags only |
| Caption length | 125–150 characters optimal; first 2 lines must hook | 150–300 words; this audience reads longer captions on Facebook |
| Paid boost priority | Reels in awareness phase | Event post boost in conversion week |
