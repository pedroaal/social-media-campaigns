# El Viñedo — Marketing Strategy

> Wine degustation event | June 27, 2024 | Manta, Ecuador
> Budget: $1,500 | Campaign: 14 weeks | Platforms: Instagram + Facebook (TikTok secondary)

---

## 1. Key Messaging Themes (5 Themes)

### Theme 1: "El vino no tiene que ser complicado"
*(Wine doesn't have to be complicated)*

**Pain point addressed:** Wine intimidation — "I don't know anything about wine and I'll feel dumb"
**Core message:** El Viñedo is the event designed for people who know nothing. That's literally the point.
**Sample copy:** "No sabes nada de vino? Bienvenido. Ese es exactamente el punto."
**When to use:** Weeks 1–6 (awareness phase — remove the barrier to entry)
**Content types:** Educational carousels, myth-busting posts, "Wine 101" explainers

---

### Theme 2: "Un sábado diferente"
*(A different Saturday)*

**Pain point addressed:** Routine, boredom, "I need something fun to do"
**Core message:** El Viñedo is the interesting thing you can say you did this month.
**Sample copy:** "¿Qué hiciste el fin de semana? Fui a probar 10 vinos y no me arrepiento."
**When to use:** Weeks 3–10 (consideration phase — position as a lifestyle choice)
**Content types:** Lifestyle photography, friend group content, social proof posts

---

### Theme 3: "Descubre tu próximo favorito"
*(Discover your next favorite)*

**Pain point addressed:** "I don't know what I like" — turns uncertainty into excitement
**Core message:** You walk out knowing at least one wine you love. And you can buy it locally.
**Sample copy:** "¿Y si el vino que más te gusta todavía no lo has probado?"
**When to use:** Weeks 4–12 (bridges awareness to consideration to conversion)
**Content types:** Wine variety previews, partner liquor store posts, discovery-angle content

---

### Theme 4: "La gente de Manta se encuentra aquí"
*(Manta's people meet here)*

**Pain point addressed:** Social isolation, desire to meet interesting people, networking
**Core message:** El Viñedo is as much about the people as the wine. Meet your city.
**Sample copy:** "Los mejores planes empiezan cuando dices sí."
**When to use:** Weeks 6–14 (builds FOMO, social proof, community feel)
**Content types:** Behind-the-scenes, attendee spotlights, partner features, community content

---

### Theme 5: "Tu lugar está esperando"
*(Your spot is waiting)*

**Pain point addressed:** Procrastination, "I'll get tickets later" — scarcity and urgency
**Core message:** Spots are limited. The people who went to the last one got it. Don't wait.
**Sample copy:** "Quedan pocos cupos. Junio 27, Manta. ¿Vienes?"
**When to use:** Weeks 11–14 only (conversion phase — don't use urgency too early or it loses effect)
**Content types:** Direct promotional posts, countdown content, ticket CTAs

## 2. Content Mix Strategy

### Content Mix (24 posts over 14 weeks)

| Content Type | % | # Posts | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Educational** ("Wine 101", myths, varietals) | 30% | 7 | Directly addresses intimidation barrier; high save/share rate; builds brand authority |
| **Experiential** (event setup, atmosphere, tasting moments) | 25% | 6 | Sells the experience visually; converts browsing into desire |
| **Social/Lifestyle** (friend groups, "a different Saturday") | 20% | 5 | Most shareable content type; drives organic reach and tagging |
| **Partner Spotlights** (liquor stores, restaurants, importers) | 17% | 4 | Fulfills secondary objective; triggers partner cross-promotion |
| **Direct CTA/Promotional** (ticket push, urgency, countdown) | 8% | 2 | Used sparingly so it lands hard when deployed |

**Why this ratio works:**
- The 30% educational anchors the brand as genuinely helpful, not just promotional
- Experiential + social together (45%) do the heavy lifting on desire and shareability
- Partner content (17%) activates the partnership network without feeling like ads
- Hard CTAs are rare by design — scarcity of urgency makes it more effective

## 3. Monthly Campaign Overview

### Month 1 (Late March → April): **Brand Awareness**
**Goal:** Build following, establish brand voice, remove intimidation barrier
**Primary theme:** Theme 1 — "El vino no tiene que ser complicado"
**Secondary theme:** Theme 2 — "Un sábado diferente"
**Posts:** 8 posts (Weeks 1–4)
**Content focus:**
- 3 educational posts (wine basics, myths, "red vs. white explained")
- 2 lifestyle/social posts (what the event atmosphere looks like)
- 2 partner intro posts (meet the liquor store partners, meet the wine importer)
- 1 soft announcement post ("Something is coming to Manta. June 27.")

**Tone:** Teasing, educational, curious — not selling yet
**CTAs:** "Follow for more", "Save this", "Tag someone who loves wine"
**No ticket push yet** — audience doesn't trust the brand enough to buy

---

### Month 2 (May): **Consideration & Desire Building**
**Goal:** Deepen engagement, build FOMO, showcase the experience
**Primary theme:** Theme 3 — "Descubre tu próximo favorito"
**Secondary theme:** Theme 4 — "La gente de Manta se encuentra aquí"
**Posts:** 8 posts (Weeks 5–8)
**Content focus:**
- 2 educational posts (specific wines at the event, what to expect)
- 3 experiential posts (venue preview, what the tasting format looks like, behind-scenes)
- 2 social proof posts (testimonials if possible, or "what people are saying")
- 1 influencer collaboration post

**Tone:** Enthusiastic, specific, FOMO-building
**CTAs:** "Grab your spot before they go", "Link in bio for tickets"
**Start** soft ticket promotion mid-May

---

### Month 3 / Pre-Event (June 1–27): **Conversion & Urgency**
**Goal:** Convert interest to ticket sales, maximize attendance
**Primary theme:** Theme 5 — "Tu lugar está esperando"
**Secondary theme:** Theme 4 (community/social proof)
**Posts:** 8 posts + increased Stories (Weeks 9–14)
**Content focus:**
- 2 direct promotional posts (ticket availability, what's included)
- 2 countdown posts (2 weeks out, 1 week out)
- 2 social/FOMO posts (group photos, "who's coming?" content)
- 1 partner spotlight (final push on liquor store CTA)
- 1 final event-week post (day-before or day-of)

**Tone:** Urgent but not panicked, excited, inclusive
**CTAs:** Hard ticket CTAs — "Últimos cupos", "Consigue tu entrada ahora"
**Increase frequency:** 3x/week in Weeks 11–13, daily Stories in event week

## 4. Platform-Specific Strategy

### Instagram Strategy

**Content Format Priority:**
1. **Reels** (15–30s): Behind-the-scenes, event atmosphere, wine pouring — highest reach
2. **Carousels** (5–8 slides): Educational content, "5 wines you'll try", partner features — highest saves
3. **Stories**: Daily in final 3 weeks — countdown, polls ("Red or white?"), Q&A, ticket link
4. **Static Posts**: Brand consistency, quote cards, announcement posts

**Hashtag Strategy:**
- 10–15 hashtags per post
- Mix: 3 local geo (#Manta, #Manabí, #Ecuador) + 4–5 wine/food + 3–4 lifestyle/event
- Alternate sets to avoid shadowban patterns
- Place hashtags in caption (not comments) for this audience size

**Stories Cadence:**
- Awareness phase: 2–3x/week Stories
- Consideration phase: 4–5x/week Stories
- Event week: Daily Stories + countdown sticker

**Instagram-Specific CTAs:**
- "Link in bio for tickets" (primary conversion path)
- "Save this post" (educational content)
- "Tag someone to come with" (reach expansion)

---

### Facebook Strategy

**Why Facebook matters for this campaign:**
The 35–50 segment in Manta is heavily active on Facebook. Event RSVPs, shares, and community discovery happen here. Facebook Events specifically drive event discovery.

**Content Approach:**
- Longer captions than Instagram (150–300 words) — this audience reads on Facebook
- Share every Instagram post to Facebook with adapted, expanded copy
- Create a **Facebook Event page** for June 27 — invite followers, encourage sharing
- Post in relevant Manta groups (with permission): foodies, expats, professionals

**Facebook-Specific Tactics:**
1. **Facebook Event**: Create 8 weeks before; update weekly; invite all followers
2. **Facebook Ads**: Targeted to Manta + Guayaquil/Quito radius, ages 28–52, interests: wine, food, events, lifestyle
3. **Boosted Posts**: Boost 2–3 key posts with $30–50 each (educational and event announcement posts)
4. **Group Outreach**: Post in "Manta" community groups with organic, non-spammy event announcements

## 5. Budget Allocation ($1,500 Total)

### Allocation Framework

| Category | Amount | % | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Facebook/Instagram Ads** | $600 | 40% | Paid reach is essential — organic alone won't hit target audience at scale |
| **Influencer Fees** | $400 | 27% | 2–3 micro-influencers (10k–50k followers) at $100–200 each — higher ROI than macro |
| **Content Creation** (photography/video) | $300 | 20% | Professional shoot: 2–3 hours, 1 photographer — foundation for all 14 weeks of content |
| **Design** (graphics, branded templates) | $150 | 10% | Canva Pro or local designer for branded carousel templates, event graphics |
| **Print/Physical** (QR flyers, in-store materials) | $50 | 3% | Minimal print — QR codes in partner locations |

### Ad Spend Breakdown ($600)

| Ad Type | Amount | When | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Facebook Event Promotion | $150 | Weeks 3–8 | Event page RSVPs, awareness |
| Instagram Reach Campaign | $150 | Weeks 5–10 | Follower growth, brand awareness |
| Instagram/Facebook Conversion Ads | $200 | Weeks 10–14 | Direct ticket sales |
| Boosted Posts (3–4 key posts) | $100 | Throughout | Amplify best-performing organic content |

### Influencer Strategy ($400)
- **Tier 1** (1 influencer, $150–200): Manta lifestyle creator, 20k–50k followers — event attendance + 2–3 Stories + 1 feed post
- **Tier 2** (1–2 influencers, $100 each): Manta or coastal Ecuador food/lifestyle creators, 5k–20k followers — event attendance + 1 post each
- **Metrics to require**: Story views, post reach, link clicks — get these in the agreement

### Maximizing $1,500 ROI
- Lead with organic content (free) for first 4 weeks to build authentic base
- Only boost posts that are already performing well organically (don't pay to promote weak content)
- Negotiate with influencers: free event access + bottles of featured wine reduces cash outlay
- Negotiate with partners: co-funded promotions reduce your effective ad spend

## 6. Optimal Posting Schedule (Confirmed)

### Weekly Posting Schedule

| Day | Platform | Time (ECT) | Content Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wednesday** | Instagram | 6:30 PM | Educational or Lifestyle | Primary Instagram slot — highest weekday evening engagement |
| **Wednesday** | Facebook | 9:30 AM | Adapted version of IG post | Morning slot performs for Facebook's older demo |
| **Friday or Saturday** | Instagram | 6:00 PM | Experiential or Social | Weekend-planning mindset |
| **Friday or Saturday** | Facebook | 7:30 PM | Event-focused or partner spotlight | Evening sharing behavior |

### Campaign Cadence by Phase

| Phase | Weeks | Posts/Week | Stories/Week |
|---|---|---|---|
| Awareness | 1–4 | 2 | 2–3 |
| Consideration | 5–10 | 2 | 3–4 |
| Ramp-up | 11–13 | 3 | 5–6 |
| Event Week | 14 | 5 | Daily |

### Reasoning
- **Wednesday 6:30 PM Instagram**: Peak engagement for working professionals post-dinner
- **Wednesday 9:30 AM Facebook**: Facebook's older demographic browses before work tasks; event shares happen here
- **Friday/Saturday 6 PM Instagram**: Pre-weekend browsing peak — lifestyle content performs best
- **Avoid**: Mondays (post-weekend low), Sunday evenings (low intent for event content), early mornings except Facebook
