### Risk Mitigation

#### Trigger-Based Escalation Protocol

| Risk | Escalation Trigger | Escalation Response | Owner |
|------|--------------------|---------------------|-------|
| Trainer resistance to check-in protocol | Any trainer with <50% Day 1 completion rate after 2 weeks | Manager holds 1:1 with trainer within 3 days; frames protocol as client-retention credential; Manager participates in 1 check-in alongside trainer as demonstration | Manager |
| Cleaning checklists become performative (filled but not executed) | WhatsApp mess reports increase for 2 consecutive weeks despite high checklist completion | Manager increases audits from 2/day to 4/day for 1 week; conducts unannounced physical inspection of zone immediately after checklist was signed; verbal warning to responsible staff member | Manager |
| Private trainers leave over zone policy | Any private trainer verbally indicates intent to leave during Week 6–8 negotiations | Manager pauses negotiation; schedules individual meeting with Gym Owner present within 48 hours; differentiates high-value vs. problematic trainers; prioritizes retaining top revenue contributors | Gym Owner + Manager |
| Off-peak incentives do not shift arrival patterns | Week 4 hourly headcount shows no measurable shift in off-peak attendance vs. Week 2 baseline | Accept peak congestion management (floor captain + rotation signs) as primary lever; suspend small perk program to avoid sunk cost; redirect energy to improving peak-hour experience | Manager |
| New member check-ins falling below target | Day 1 completion rate drops below 70% for any 2-week period | Reception staff verbally reminds new member of trainer name and Day 1 appointment at signup; trainer receives automatic reminder via WhatsApp group the morning of each scheduled contact | Manager |
| WhatsApp capacity updates abandoned | Status coverage drops below 50% for any 3-day period | Manager assigns backup Reception staff for that shift; posts coverage schedule at front desk | Manager |
